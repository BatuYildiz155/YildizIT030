Insight 1: Mobile vs Desktop Engagement

What I found: Most users visited the website using mobile devices, with mobile traffic making up over 65% of users. However, mobile users had a much lower engagement rate compared to desktop users.

Why it surprised me: I expected mobile users to engage more because most people browse and shop online from their phones today. Instead, desktop users interacted with the website far more often.

What I would do about it: I would improve the mobile version of the website by making navigation simpler, speeding up page loading times, and optimizing the checkout experience for phones. Since most visitors are on mobile devices, improving usability could increase engagement and sales.

Insight 2: Organic Video Traffic

What I found: Visitors coming from Organic Video had the highest average engagement time on the website, staying for over five minutes on average. However, only a very small number of users came from this traffic source.

Why it surprised me: I expected search engines or social media to create the highest engagement, but video traffic kept users interested for much longer even though there were fewer visitors.

What I would do about it: I would invest more into video marketing on platforms like YouTube, TikTok, and Instagram Reels. Since video visitors appear to be highly engaged, increasing video content could bring in more quality traffic and improve conversions.
